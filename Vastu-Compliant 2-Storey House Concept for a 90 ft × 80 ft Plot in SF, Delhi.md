# Vastu-Compliant 2-Storey House Concept for a 90 ft × 80 ft Plot in SF, Delhi
## Executive Overview
This report develops a conceptual architectural and interior layout for a two-storey house on a 90 ft (depth) × 80 ft (width) plot in SF, Delhi, prioritising strong Vastu compliance while maintaining modern spatial comfort and climate-responsive design for Delhi’s composite climate. The design assumes a cardinally oriented plot with the road and primary access on the north side; if the actual orientation differs, the same logic can be rotated to align with the true north–south axis after on-site compass verification.[^1][^2][^3][^4][^5]

The concept locates the built mass towards the south and west to keep the north-east lighter and open, places the main entrance towards the north-east sector of the north side, uses the south-east for the kitchen, and positions the master bedroom in the south-west, in line with widely accepted Vastu guidance. Interior zoning, staircase placement, and colour/material choices are tuned to Vastu principles while also using passive-cooling strategies like shaded courtyards, deep verandahs, and an east–west building orientation with a longer north and south façade for better thermal performance in Delhi.[^6][^4][^7][^5][^8][^1]
## Key Vastu Principles Used
### Directional roles and room placement
Contemporary summaries of Vastu shastra for homes converge on a few robust direction–function pairings:[^1][^6][^4]

- Main entrance: Favour north, east, or north-east; avoid south-west.
- Pooja room: Prefer north-east (Ishan corner); west is a secondary option.[^9][^4][^1]
- Kitchen: Prefer south-east (Agni), with cooking facing east; west is a secondary option.[^6][^1]
- Master bedroom: Prefer south-west for stability and rest.[^1][^6]
- Living room/drawing room: Prefer north or east sides; avoid heavy clutter in the north-east.[^6][^1]
- Toilets/bathrooms: Favour north-west or west; avoid north-east and exact centre.[^1][^6]
- Staircase: Prefer south, south-west, or west zones; avoid north-east; odd number of risers and clockwise ascent are typically recommended.[^10][^11][^12][^1]

These placements underpin the conceptual zoning of both floors.
### Plot and building form
Vastu and contemporary architectural practice both favour simple, regular plot utilisation—square or close-to-rectangular footprints divided using a directional grid (Vastu Purusha Mandala), with the north-east portion kept lighter or open where possible. A 90 ft × 80 ft plot allows generous setbacks and landscape design; to align with Delhi’s climate, the primary built block is oriented with its long axis east–west so that the main external walls face north and south, which reduces summer solar gain and improves winter performance.[^2][^9][^4][^5]
### Legal and planning caveat for SF
SF is an affluent but legally complex, largely unauthorised colony in South Delhi, where courts have repeatedly noted the illegality of many residential constructions and imposed restrictions, with selective enforcement on new work. Any real project must therefore be validated against the most current Delhi Master Plan, zoning notifications, and legal advice, and the concept in this report should be treated strictly as a design study, not a permit-ready blueprint.[^13][^14]
## Site and Orientation Assumptions
### Base assumptions
- Plot size: 90 ft (north–south depth) × 80 ft (east–west width).
- Road and main approach: On north side, making it a north-facing plot.
- Climate: Delhi’s composite climate with hot summers, cool winters, and strong solar exposure.
- Desired form: 2-storey villa with landscaped setbacks and outdoor living.

Delhi-focused bioclimatic guidance suggests orienting the main building mass such that the longer dimension runs east–west, exposing the shorter façades to the intense morning and afternoon sun while allowing controlled north and south light with shading. Courtyard and veranda elements, similar to those used in climate-responsive Delhi institutions and farmhouses, help to temper heat and support outdoor living under shade.[^7][^5][^8]
### High-level site layout
Conceptual north-facing plot layout (plan view, not to scale):

- Front (north) 20–25 ft depth: Entry court, driveway, visitor parking, soft landscaping, and a light water feature towards the north-east.
- Central 40–50 ft: Main house block (approximately 60 ft wide × 45–50 ft deep), leaving side setbacks for light and cross-ventilation.
- Rear (south) 15–20 ft: Private garden with shaded seating, kitchen garden, and service yard.

This arrangement keeps the built mass somewhat south-shifted and avoids heavy built-up in the north-east, in line with Vastu’s orientation guidance and climate-driven recommendations to shade south façades with overhangs and vegetation.[^4][^5]

The ground floor balances public and semi-public functions while keeping the most sacred and stable zones—pooja and parents/master suite—aligned with Vastu.

### Ground floor spaces and flow
1. **Entry sequence (north and north-east)**
   - Gate and pedestrian entry positioned in the north or north-east third of the front boundary, aligned to auspicious padas on the north side as per Vastu door mapping.[^2]
   - A landscaped forecourt with low-height planting and a small water feature kept towards the north-east supports the idea of a light, open Ishan corner.[^1][^4]
   - A covered entrance porch leads to a foyer with a clear view of the formal living but not directly through to private spaces, maintaining both Vastu energy containment and privacy.

2. **Formal living / drawing room (north-east to east)**
   - The living room occupies much of the north-east and east front zone, with seating oriented so that primary sofas allow guests to sit facing east or north.[^6][^1]
   - Large openings on the north and east allow diffuse light; external shading (deep chajjas, pergolas) controls glare and heat, consistent with climate-responsive design guidance for Delhi.[^7][^5]
   - The pooja room is integrated as a dedicated enclosed or semi-open niche at the extreme north-east, with the deity placed so that worshippers face east while praying.[^9][^4][^1]

3. **Staircase and circulation (west / south-west)**
   - A solid staircase block is sited in the west or south-west quadrant, rising clockwise with an odd number of steps per flight to align with common staircase Vastu recommendations.[^10][^15][^11][^12]
   - The stair core also acts as a thermal buffer on the hotter west side, reducing direct heat into living areas.
   - Under-stair storage is acceptable here; Vastu typically advises against pooja or bathrooms under stairs, which this layout avoids.[^12]

4. **Kitchen and informal dining (south-east)**
   - The kitchen is placed in the south-east, with the stove on the south or south-east wall so that the cook faces east while cooking.[^1][^6]
   - Sinks and water-intensive counters are pulled slightly towards the north or north-east side of the kitchen to separate fire and water elements.[^6]
   - An attached utility yard or service corridor along the east/south-east exterior provides laundry and outdoor cooking support while keeping heavy service out of the north-east and centre.

5. **Dining and family room (south / centre)**
   - A central or slightly south-biased dining area connects kitchen, living, and rear garden, allowing the family to sit facing east or north where possible.[^6]
   - Sliding doors or large windows open to a shaded rear patio, encouraging natural ventilation along the north–south axis.

6. **Ground floor master/parents’ bedroom (south-west)**
   - The primary ground-floor bedroom sits firmly in the south-west with the bed headboard on the south or west wall, so sleepers face east or north.[^1][^6]
   - Attached toilet and dressing are nestled on the west or south side of this suite, keeping toilet fixtures away from the south-west corner itself and avoiding the north-east.[^1]

7. **Guest/office and powder room (north-west / west)**
   - A flexible guest bedroom or home office occupies the north-west, which is suited for guests and work that does not need to be permanent.[^1]
   - A common powder room is placed in the west or north-west, away from the north-east and centre; this follows Vastu advice favouring toilets in west or north-west zones.[^6][^1]
## First Floor Concept Plan
### Overall Vastu zoning on first floor
The first floor primarily hosts family bedrooms, a family lounge, possibly a home theatre or study, and terrace gardens, while respecting the underlying ground-floor directional logic.

- **South-West:** Master bedroom (if the ground floor suite is for parents, first floor SW can hold the younger couple’s master suite).
- **South and South-East:** Children’s bedrooms or a study, with care not to overload the south-east with toilets or clutter.
- **North-West:** Guest bedroom or children’s bedroom.
- **North and North-East:** Family lounge, study corner, or open terrace garden, keeping these zones relatively light.

The staircase continues into this floor in the west/south-west, maintaining consistent Vastu treatment.
### First floor spaces
1. **Master suite (south-west)**
   - The main master suite sits in the south-west quadrant, repeating the principle of a heavy, stable function in this direction.[^1][^6]
   - The bed is placed with a solid wall behind (south or west), avoiding beams overhead and keeping toilets to the west or south side of the suite.[^6][^1]
   - A walk-in wardrobe and ensuite can buffer the outer walls to improve acoustics and thermal comfort.

2. **Children’s bedrooms (south, south-east, north-west)**
   - One child’s room is placed along the south or south-east, with bed head to south or east and study desk facing east or north.[^6]
   - Another child or guest room is located in the north-west, which suits dynamic, changing occupancy; attached toilets are kept within west/north-west sectors, not encroaching on north-east.[^1]

3. **Family lounge and study (north / north-east)**
   - A family lounge or informal sitting space is in the north or east side, with a more contemplative study or library corner leaning towards the north-east while still keeping that corner uncluttered.[^1][^6]
   - This area can open onto a partly covered terrace over the front porch, benefiting from north light and breezes while using pergolas and planters for shading.[^7][^5]

4. **Terraces and roof gardens**
   - The first floor steps back in parts to create terraces, primarily towards the north and east, which keep the north-east open and breathe light into the lower floor.[^4][^5]
   - Heavier rooftop elements, such as water tanks, are located towards the south-west, which aligns with Vastu suggestions to put overhead tanks in that zone.[^1]
## Climate-Responsive Strategies for Delhi
### Orientation and massing
Bioclimatic analysis for Delhi’s composite climate suggests that building long sides facing north and south, coupled with compact courtyard planning, reduces heat gain and allows better seasonal performance. This concept follows that recommendation by using the 80 ft width for the north and south façades and consolidating vertical circulation and services on the hotter west side.[^5]
### Shading and courtyards
Delhi examples such as India Habitat Centre and several new farmhouses demonstrate that shaded courtyards, arcades, and layered façades can significantly soften the microclimate by filtering sun and promoting air movement. Within this concept:[^7][^8]

- A semi-open internal or side courtyard is used between living and dining, drawing in light from the east/north but protected by overhangs.
- Deep verandahs or pergola-covered decks are placed along the north and east, extending living spaces into the landscape while cutting direct solar penetration.
- Trees, water features, and planters are concentrated in the north and east gardens to cool incoming breezes and keep the north-east light in both Vastu and thermal terms.[^4][^7]
### Envelope and materials
For Delhi, high thermal mass walls and roofs with over 8-hour time lag and good insulation are recommended to delay heat ingress and stabilise indoor temperatures. The design would therefore use:[^5]

- Brick or concrete masonry walls with external insulation or cavity walls.
- High-performance glazing with external shading rather than relying on dark reflective glass.
- Well-insulated flat roofs with green patches or reflective finishes.
## Interior Design with Vastu and Minimalism
### Colour palette by room and direction
Modern paint and interior guidance maps Vastu energies onto colours, suggesting light neutrals and gentle tones that harmonise with directional elements.[^16][^17][^18][^19][^20]

- **Living room (north/east):** Whites, creams, light yellows or soft greens, which align with air and water elements and support sociable, fresh energy.[^19][^20][^16]
- **Master bedroom (south-west):** Beige, cream, light brown, or muted earthy tones that emphasise stability and grounding.[^18]
- **Children’s bedrooms (south, east, north-west):** Light blue, pastel green, or soft peach, avoiding very intense reds or neons; headboards on solid walls.[^17][^18]
- **Kitchen (south-east):** Warm neutrals, soft orange, or brick red accents integrated with cream cabinets to represent fire without visual clutter.[^6][^17]
- **Pooja room (north-east):** White, very light yellow, or soft blue, keeping surfaces clean, reflective, and uncluttered.[^4][^16]

These hues align with Vastu’s elemental associations and can be expressed in a minimalist way—plain walls, clean-lined furniture, and a limited, cohesive palette.
### Furniture placement and circulation
- Heavy furniture such as large sofas and storage units is anchored to south and west walls, keeping north and east more open, consistent with both Vastu and daylighting logic.[^6][^4]
- Beds are kept away from exact room centres and aligned with headboards on south or west walls to maintain stable backing and avoid sleeping with heads towards north.[^6]
- Workstations and study desks are oriented so occupants face east or north for focus and productivity.[^6]
- Mirrors are avoided directly opposite the bed and near the main door, as many Vastu practitioners consider these to disturb rest and energy flow, though this point is more interpretive and should be confirmed with a dedicated consultant for strict adherence.[^1][^6]
## Staircase and Service Core Detailing
Staircase Vastu places emphasis on three parameters: direction, rotation, and step count.[^10][^15][^11][^12]

- **Direction:** Locate the staircase in south, south-west, west, or north-west; avoid north-east.
- **Rotation:** Use a clockwise ascent where possible.
- **Steps:** Maintain odd numbers of steps in each flight (e.g., 9, 11, 15) so that the ascent begins and ends on the right foot.

The proposed west/south-west stair core follows these rules and also consolidates vertical shaft elements such as service ducts, risers, and storage, leaving north-east and centre open.
## Water, Tanks, and Services
Vastu-oriented zoning for water suggests underground water tanks or wells in the north-east, and overhead water tanks in the south-west, with toilets in the west or north-west.[^1][^6]

- An underground or semi-sunken water tank is placed under the front garden’s north-east, integrated with landscape design.
- Overhead tanks and solar thermal equipment are grouped in the south-west of the roof, kept visually integrated into parapets.
- Toilets are organised in vertical stacks in west and north-west zones to simplify plumbing and maintain Vastu consistency.
## Flexibility and Rotations
The directional logic used here can be rotated if the actual plot faces east, west, or south instead of north, with the functional relationships preserved: pooja in the Ishan corner, kitchen in the Agni corner, master in the south-west, and stairs in a heavy western/southern quadrant. When doing this in practice, accurate plotting of cardinal directions and degrees—preferably using a polar coordinate approach to cast the Vastu grid—is necessary to avoid the common mistake of blindly copying generic plans.[^1][^2][^9][^4]
## Limitations and Next Steps
This concept synthesises widely accepted Vastu guidelines, generic north-facing house planning advice, and Delhi-specific climatic design principles, but individual practitioners differ on details and remedies, and legal constraints in SF are significant. For an actual project, the next steps should include a licensed Delhi architect’s survey, legal consultation on land-use permissibility, and, if desired, a session with a professional Vastu consultant who can map exact degrees and provide corrections for any unavoidable deviations.[^1][^13][^2][^14][^6][^4][^5]
